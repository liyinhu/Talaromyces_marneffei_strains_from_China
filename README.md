# Talaromyces marneffei strains from China
Talaromyces marneffei is an emerging fungal pathogen endemic to Southeast Asia, causing life-threatening invasive mycoses in immunocompromised populations and presenting steady increases in incidence over recent decades. Although several genomic studies of T. marneffei have been reported, most have relied on individual clinical isolates, and animal-derived strains have rarely been integrated into population-scale analyses, hindering our exploration of its evolutionary lineages and genomic composition. This project collected 40 Talaromyces marneffei strains between 2020 and 2025 from Guangxi, Guangdong, and Jiangxi provinces in southern China, and delineated the population structure, evolutionary relationships, and genomic composition of T. marneffei at a global scale. We present the major bioinformatic scripts used in the study. <br>
* Gene prediction
* Genomic variation detection
* Genomic variation annotation
* Population clustering
* Nucleotide diversity analysis
* Construction of the pangenome
* Pangenome analysis
